Neil deGrasse Tyson

Neil deGrasse Tyson is an American astrophysicist, cosmologist, author, and science communicator. He is the Frederick P. Rose Director of the Hayden Planetarium at the Rose Center for Earth and Space in New York City. Tyson's research has focused on stellar evolution, galactic astronomy, and cosmology. He has written numerous books and has hosted several television shows, including the Emmy-nominated "Cosmos: A Spacetime Odyssey."

Life Events

  • 1958: Neil deGrasse Tyson was born on October 5, 1958 in New York City.
  • 1988: Tyson earned his doctorate in astrophysics from Columbia University.
  • 1996: Tyson became the Director of the Hayden Planetarium.
  • 2006: Neil deGrasse Tyson changed the world by contributing to the research that established Pluto as a dwarf planet.
  • 2014: Tyson hosted the television series "Cosmos: A Spacetime Odyssey," a follow-up to Carl Sagan's "Cosmos: A Personal Voyage."
  • 2017: Tyson was awarded the Stephen Hawking Medal for Science Communication.
